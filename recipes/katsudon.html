Katsudon

Pork Cutlet Rice Bowl

Description

A popular Japanese rice bowl, or "donburi" as it's called. It features a fried pork cutlet, simmered in a mixture of eggs, onions, and a savory sauce.

Ingrediants

For the Tonkatsu

  • 2 boneless pork chops, pounded 1/2 inch thick

  • 1/2 cup flour

  • 1 egg, whisked (reserve remaining)

  • 2 cups panko bread crumbs

For the Katsudon

  • 2 cups of rice

  • 1/2 cup chicken stock

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce

  • 1 tablespoon EACH mirin, and rice vinegar (or sake)

  • 1 tablespoon + 1 teaspoon dark brown sugar

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

  • 2 medium oinons, sliced thinly

  • 2 eggs, whisked

  • Sliced green onions and/or Japanese BBQ sauce for topping

Instructions

For the Tonkatsu

  1. Heat 1 inch of oil in a skillet over high heat to 350F

  2. Season both sides of the pork cutlet with salt and allow to sit for a few minutes.

  3. Meanwhile whisk the egg and set aside until ready.

  4. Once pork cutlet has rested, cover with flour making sure to coat it entirely. Dredge it inot your egg mixture and then the panko. Fry it in the oil until golden brown, about 8-10 minutes, before removing to a paper towel lined plate and repeat for remaining cutlet.

For the Katsudon

  1. Combine broth, soy sauce, mirin, brown sugar, and rice vinegar together in a bowl.

  2. Combine reserved egg mixture from tonkatsu with 2 eggs and whisk together. Add salt and pepper if desired.

  3. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium high heat. Add onions and saut√© till they are soft and starting to caramelize.

  4. Add the broth mixture and simmer for 3-4 minutes.

  5. Slice the tonkatsu into equal strips and carefully lay them on top of the onion mixture.

  6. Drizzle the whisked eggs all over everything. Cover and cook for about 2 minutes, or until the egg is just set.

  7. Serve over bowls of rice and garnish with green onions or Japanese BBQ sauce for an extra punch of presentation.
